How do Women engage in open data movements cross the Mekong countries?

ODL want to understand how women are engaging in open data movements across the Mekong countries. We are particularly interested in understanding why and how women participate and what some of the barriers to their participation are. SDG 1 No poverty

This page discusses SDG 1-No poverty which aims to eradicate all forms of poverty everywhere including ensuring social protection, increasing access to basic services and supporting resilience to climate-related disasters and other economic, social and environmental shocks for the poor and vulnerable. It describes an overview of how SDG 1 is localized, implemented and monitored in the context of Lao PDR.

Social development

Lao PDR has achieved a number of ambitious goals on social development. Poverty levels have halved, GDP growth averaged 7.8% over the past decade, and education and health outcomes have improved tremendously. Challenges remain, however, such as child undernutrition, declining total fertility rates, high maternal mortality rates and a high fiscal deficit.

Labor

Laos is low in terms of human capital which reflects by the number and skills of local workers. Most workers in Lao PDR are in agricultural sector. Despite this, Laos has developed a legal framework to protect its laborers, but only formal workers tend to be the most beneficial group…

Ethnic minorities and indigenous people

Lao PDR is the most ethnically diverse country in Southeast Asia. Lao people comprise four main ethno-linguistic groups: Lao-Tai (62.4 %), Mon-Khmer (23.7 %), Hmong-Iu Mien (9.7 %), and Chine-Tibetan (2.9 %), which can also be broken down to 49 ethnic groups. Many ethnic groups in Laos encounter issues of poverty, education and health, due to lifestyle and geographical difficulties.
